As development continues, I’ve started learning pixel art alongside building the game. This is the first original asset created specifically for Veilkeeper — a selection crystal used in the combat grid.

The goal wasn’t to make something perfect, but to create something readable and functional that could fit into the game’s visual language. The crystal is designed in grayscale so it can be tinted later based on context, such as player actions, enemy states, or signal strength.

The difference between the two versions highlights a few key improvements: clearer shape definition, better separation of light and shadow, and a stronger overall silhouette. Even small adjustments to shading made a noticeable impact on readability.

This process is part of a larger effort to build a consistent visual identity for Veilkeeper. Early assets like this won’t be final, but they establish direction and make it easier to refine or replace them later with more polished work.

## What improved

- Clearer crystal silhouette
- More defined light and shadow planes
- Improved readability at smaller sizes
- Stronger overall shape consistency
- Better foundation for future UI elements

There’s still a long way to go, but this is the point where the visual side of the game starts to feel real. Future Art of the Veil posts will continue to explore UI elements, assets, and the evolving look of the game.
