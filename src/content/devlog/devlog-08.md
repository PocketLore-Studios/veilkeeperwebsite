One of my goals for the demo is making sure new players can understand Veilkeeper without needing a tutorial or prior knowledge. That starts before the battle even begins.

The squad drafting phase now includes unit information cards that explain what each unit is designed to do. Instead of selecting from a list of names, players can quickly learn a unit's battlefield role, strengths, weaknesses, and overall tactical purpose before committing to their squad.

For example, the Knight is presented as a durable frontline anchor that excels at absorbing pressure and creating opportunities for the rest of the team, while also clearly communicating its limitations. The goal isn't to overwhelm players with numbers, but to give them enough context to make informed decisions.

This update doesn't change the combat mechanics themselves, but it significantly improves the first-time player experience. Small usability improvements like these make the game feel more approachable and help players focus on learning tactics instead of guessing what each unit does.

As development continues toward Alpha, I'm spending more time refining the player's first impression. Every improvement to the interface helps Veilkeeper feel a little more like a complete game and a little less like a prototype.

## Current Progress

- Added unit information cards to the squad draft screen
- Each card displays portrait, role, strengths, weaknesses, and tactical overview
- Improved first-time player onboarding before combat begins
- Continued polishing the demo experience
